04 / Stabil.otf

Typeface advertising campaign for the Stabilo point 88: The two defining features of the point 88 are its distinctive shape and wide range of colors. These two qualities form the foundation of the campaign “Stabil.otf,” demonstrating the potential of a digital extension. The typeface includes ligatures, accents, and alternates to create an individual character. In collaboration with Jiyeon Park.

05 / Neon Signs

This single-line font is inspired by neon sign bending, where reducing the number of welds is key. Fewer welds save manufacturing time, minimize the risk of leaks, and enhance the overall appearance. This technique, known as continuous bending, uses as much of a single length of tubing as possible before cutting and welding. It requires careful planning and the ability to anticipate bends ahead of sequence. That’s why this font includes around 170 glyphs and 15 contextual alternate classes.

06 / Cavalier

The typeface CAVALIER is based on a historical lettering drawing by Robert Stöcklin (born February 17, 1889, in Basel). It is therefore a composition of the new and the traditional, with careful consideration given to what should be preserved and what should be modernized. To shape a more dynamic and playful character, I designed a set of ligatures and contextual alternates.

07 / 3D Animation

This experimental 3D project reimagines the look of early CGI and screen savers from the 1980s and 1990s through the format of a DVD. The scenes were intentionally rendered “badly” in Blender, using low-quality visuals to transform technical imperfection into a surrealistic retro atmosphere.
